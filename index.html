The Empire Game

A play-by-email megagame about an empire in decline.

News Information

Object of the Game

  • The object of the empire game is to bring peace to the galaxy during a time of great strife as the old imperial dynasty falls into ruin and chaos and all the imperial houses rush to fill the power vacuum.
  • The game lasts for six months at one turn a week or 24 turns. The game will start on XX August 2020 and end on XX March 2020. We will take a week off for Thanksgiving, Christmas, and New Years.
  • Each turn represents a season or four months of game time. The entire game will cover 8 years, from IC 2180 to IC 2188.

At the end of the game points will be awarded thusly:

  • 20 points to anyone who is a member of a house which controls the majority of territory. Allied houses count each other’s territory as part of their own.
  • 10 points to any individual who is the empress or emperor of the majority held territory.
  • Tie breaker: -1 point to each member of a House for every commander or officer death suffered.

Turn Order

  1. Scenarios are released each Sunday night by 11:59pm CST. Announcements or holiday breaks will be announced at the same time. Commanders may ask questions etc.
  2. The News will be released throughout the week.
  3. Commanders deliberate with their house, brief their officers and issue orders.
  4. Officers consider their approach and transmit their final actions to the referees no later than Wednesday evening at 11:59pm CST.
  5. Late actions will have a negative effect on the success chance of the action.
  6. Orders that do not arrive by Thursday at 8am CST will cause the officer to be MIA.
  7. Referees collect final actions, deliberate, rule on actions’ outcomes, and generate new scenarios.

Rules of the Game

  • No communication with players outside your House about the game. Communication is a huge part of the game. If you want to make contact with another house, send a messenger, diplomat, or spy. Or defect.
  • This is a competitive game with a huge potential for failure, betrayal, death, and loss (we hope). As such we ask that you all be good sports and get into the spirit of the game, and compete passionately but without crossing any personal lines by becoming mean spirited or emotionally abusive.
  • If you have an in person problem with another player that you can sort out as adults, contact the referees.
